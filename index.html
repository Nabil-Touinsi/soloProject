About us

Make sure you are hungry before ordering a “lablabi”. This hearty dish, very popular in Tunisia, consists of a chickpea soup seasoned with olive oil, salt, pepper, and lots of cumin. Tasting a “lablabi” is a small ritual: you start by crumble stale bread in your terracotta bowl. The cook then pours the chickpea soup and adds a soft-boiled egg, harissa, lemon juice and a ladle of olive oil. Then it's up to you to mix all the ingredients. As an option, you can add tuna and even calf's feet (hargma). This traditional dish is best eaten when it is cold.
